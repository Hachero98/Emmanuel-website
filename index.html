About Me

EH

I am a PhD student in the Department of Computational Mathematics, where I develop machine-learning-based methods for solving differential equations. My work sits at the intersection of numerical analysis, deep learning, and scientific computing.

My primary focus is on Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) and Neural Ordinary Differential Equations (Neural ODEs) — methods that embed physical laws directly into neural network architectures, enabling data-efficient and interpretable solvers for complex PDE systems.

I am broadly interested in questions of convergence, stability, and error estimation for learned solvers, as well as their application to inverse problems in science and engineering.

Research

My work focuses on bridging classical numerical methods with modern machine learning for scientific computing.

Physics-Informed Neural Networks

PINNs encode PDE residuals directly into the training loss, allowing neural networks to serve as mesh-free solvers. I study theoretical guarantees including stability, convergence rates, and the role of collocation point sampling on approximation error.

$\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{data}} + \lambda\,\mathcal{L}_{\text{PDE}} + \mathcal{L}_{\text{BC}}$

Neural Ordinary Differential Equations

Neural ODEs parameterize the derivative of a hidden state using a neural network, enabling continuous-depth models and elegant treatment of dynamical systems. I investigate their expressivity, adjoint-based training stability, and connections to classical ODE solvers.

$\frac{d\mathbf{h}(t)}{dt} = f_\theta(\mathbf{h}(t),\, t)$

Operator Learning & Inverse Problems

Beyond forward solvers, I work on learning solution operators that map PDE parameters to solutions, and on recovering unknown coefficients or source terms from sparse observations — inverse problems ubiquitous in imaging, geophysics, and biology.

$\mathcal{G}_\theta : \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{U}, \quad a \mapsto u$

Numerical Methods & Error Analysis

A central theme of my research is the rigorous analysis of learned solvers. I develop error estimates that account for approximation, optimization, and generalization error, connecting the classical numerical analysis framework to the statistical learning setting.

$\|u - u_\theta\| \leq \varepsilon_{\text{approx}} + \varepsilon_{\text{opt}} + \varepsilon_{\text{gen}}$
